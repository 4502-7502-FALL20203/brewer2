7 Best Things to Do in Columbia, MO

A ranked and highly reviewed list of places and things to do/go in Columbia, MO. Whether you’re visiting or living here, these are a few things you can add to your itinerary.

Rock Bridge Memorial State Park

Just minutes from Columbia, Rock Bridge Memorial State Park gives visitors the chance to scramble, hike and bicycle through a scenic environment – and lets them peek into Missouri’s underworld. The park contains some of the most popular hiking trails in the state and also offers solitude while hiking in the Gans Creek Wild Area. Visitors can also see a large cave system with its rock bridge, sinkholes, a spring and underground stream at the Devil's Icebox. You can explore Connor's Cave in the light of the opening for a taste of the underground world.

Stephens Lake Park

This is a great park in Columbia for walking (with or without dogs), relaxing by the lake, taking kids swimming, or having a picnic. They have a couple of play areas for kids, and they also have shelters you can rent or use if not in use. Great park in Columbia!

Shelter Gardens

Although this is a small garden/park area, it is a great place to sit & visit with someone, read a book, have a picnic, walk, or relax. The trees, lawns, and plants are well-maintained. The trees and most plants have a name label and there are many plants. The koi pool is in a shaded area, so you can easily spend a long time watching the fish.

Museum of Art and Archaeology

Excellent collection of antiquities from ancient civilizations, including Greek and Roman coins. Excellent works of art from Renaissance to contemporary. Interesting collection of casts of Greek and Roman statues. Very informative and helpful documents are available.

Eagle Bluffs Conservation Area

Eagle Bluffs Wildlife Area is located just 10 miles from Columbia Missouri and is located adjacent to the Missouri River. Eagle Bluffs Wildlife Area is a unique wetland complex that can be seasonally flooded using polished sewage wastewater from the City of Columbia or water pumped from the adjacent river. The area is popular for hiking, biking, bird-watching, or hunting during prescribed seasons. Hunting is allowed to holders of a lottery-system application in addition to some allotted positions for stand-by hunters if all of the reserved positions are not filled.
